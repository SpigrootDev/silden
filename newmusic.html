Music that ended a war:

The Liberian Civil War: From 1989 to 2003, a brutal civil war raged in Liberia. Women suffered particularly from the atrocities.

"Women of Liberia Mass Action for Peace"

This movement, led by Leymah Gbowee, used music to fight for peace.

Musical protest

The women sang peace songs at protests and during peace talks to underline their demands for a ceasefire.

Success

The pressure led to the end of the civil war in 2003.

Quote from Leymah Gbowee

"We sang to show we were serious. We sang because words were not enough to express our desire for peace."

The Psychology of New Music: How Rap, Pop and Hip-Hop Affect the Psyche

The music genres rap, pop and hip-hop have had a significant impact on the psyche of millions of people worldwide since their creation. These styles of music not only affect our emotions and cognitive processes, but also our social interactions and animal behavior.

Emotional impact of rap, pop and hip hop

  • Rap music and cathsrsis:
    • Rap provides a platform to express aggression, frustration and social injustices.
    • Travis (2013): Rap helps adolescents process their emotions and reduce stress.
    • Tyson (2002): Rap music promotes a sense of belonging and self-efficacy.
  • Pop music and positive emotions:
    • Greasley & Lamont (2011): Pop music often leads to positive emotions such as joy and happiness.
    • Brattico et al. (2011): Listening to pop music increases positive emotions and reduces stress.
  • Hip-hop and empowerment:
    • Hooks (2016): Hip-hop increases self-confidence and gives listeners a sense of empowerment.

Cognitive effects:rap, pop and hip-hop

  • Rap and verbal skills:
    • Cutler (2007): Listening to rap music improves verbal skills and language comprehension.
  • Pop music and memory:
    • Wallace (1994): Pop music promotes memory performance and helps with recalling information.
  • Hip-hop and problem-solving skills:
    • Liu et al. (2012): Freestyle rap promotes creative thinking and improves problem-solving skills.

Experiments on humans

  • Rap music and stress reduction:
    • Miranda & Claes (2009): Listening to rap music led to a significant reduction in anxiety and stress in adolescents.
  • Pop music and motivation:
    • Karageorghis et al. (2010): Pop music increases motivation and helps to endure longer exercise sessions.
  • Hip-hop and therapeutic effects:
    • Elligan (2004): Hip-hop therapy helps adolescents express their emotions and manage behavioral problems.

Animal stuidies: reactions to rap, pop and hip-hop

  • Rap and rats:
    • Smith & Williams (2010): Rats that listened to rap music showed increased activity and alertness.
  • Pop music and cows:
    • Alworth et al. (2012): Cows that listened to pop music produced more milk and showed less stress.
  • Hip-hop and dogs:
    • Wells (2002): Dogs responded to hip-hop with increased alertness and activity.

Social and cultural aspects

  • Rap music and community:
    • Rose (1994): Hip-hop culture strengthens the sense of community and creates a sense of belonging.
  • Pop music and social trends:
    • Pop music often reflects social trends and provides a universal language.

Conclusion

Rap, pop and hip hop are not just musical expressions, but powerful tools for emotional release, social connection and cognitive development. Their influences on emotions, cognitive abilities and social behavior are profound, in both humans and animals.

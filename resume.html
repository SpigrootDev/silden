Clonclusion:

In summary, music was a powerful driving force for social change from 1900 to 2010. The psychological impact of music, its ability to evoke emotions and shape identities, has shaped generations. The Beatles embodied a new youthful energy that challenged norms and became a symbol of cultural awakening. Their music spoke to the youth's need for freedom and self-determination, inspiring a global youth movement and changing social conventions. In parallel, many women used their voices as a means of protest and empowerment to fight for equality. Singers such as Aretha Franklin and Joan Baez became role models and catalysts for social change by demanding justice and challenging social norms with their music. The psychological power of music to unite people, direct emotions and spark social movements shows how music has changed the world time and time again - and will continue to change it.

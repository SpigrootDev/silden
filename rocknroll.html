Rock 'n' Roll

Background

Rock 'n' roll developed from rhythm & blues and country music. In the 1950s it became a symbol of youth culture and rebellion.

Example:

Elvis Presley inspired a whole generation of young people, even behind the Iron Curtain.

The Psychology of Rock 'n' Roll: Insights into its Effects on the Psyche

Born in the 1950s, rock 'n' roll combines driving rhythms, electric guitars and catchy melodies and has revolutionized the music landscape. But its effects go far beyond music and also affect our emotions, cognitive processes and even animal behavior.

Rock 'n' Roll and Emotions: Energy and Catharsis

  • Rebellion and Freedom

    • Rock 'n' Roll is closely linked to feelings of freedom and excitement.

    • Study by Rentfrow & Gosling (2003): Rock music listeners often score high on the dimensions of openness and extraversion.
    • Louder rock music can surprisingly reduce negative emotions and improve mood (Sharman & Dingle, 2015).

Rock 'n' Roll and cognetive performance

  • Promoting alertness and motivation:

    • Schellenberg (2005): Fast, rhythmic rock music can increase reaction time and concentration during cognitive tasks.
  • Improving performance:

    • The energetic nature of rock music stimulates the sympathetic nervous system, thus promoting motivation and performance.

Animal Studies: Responses to rock music

  • Rats:
    • McCutcheon & Hocking (1997): Rats that listened to rock music were more active and moved more than those that listened to quieter music.
  • Cows
    • Käser et al. (2010): Responses varied – some cows reacted with stress, others ignored the music or got used to it.

Social and cultural role of rock 'n' roll

  • Cultural significace:

Rock 'n' roll was the sound of youth rebellion and helped overcome cultural barriers.

    • Musicians such as Elvis Presley and Chuck Berry helped to break down prejudices and promote cultural diversity.

Rock 'n' roll and menatal health

  • Therapeutic effect:
    • Baker & Bor (2008): Rock music promotes social bonding and helps with emotion regulation.
  • Music therapy:
    • In music therapy, rock 'n' roll helps to manage negative emotions and increase well-being.

Conclusion

Rock 'n' roll is not just a genre of music. It acts as a powerful tool for emotional release, stress relief and motivation. In addition, it plays an important role in music therapy and promotes mental health. Its cultural significance and ability to form communities make it a unique tool for the psyche and life.

Metal

Background

Metal emerged in the late 1960s and early 1970s as a reaction to social injustices and political crises.

Criticism of war and violence

Protest music: Many heavy metal bands criticized war and violence. Songs like “War Pigs” by Black Sabbath are indictments of politicians who wage wars. Sociological impact: Heavy metal became a platform for young people to express their fears and anger.

Music as Cultural Identity

In many parts of the world, music serves as an expression of cultural identity and resistance to political and social oppression. Genres such as reggae, hip-hop and electronic music have become symbols of resistance in certain regions and have helped people share their stories and experiences.

The Psychology of Metal: effects and experiments

Heavy metal - a music genre that has polarized since the late 1960s. Known for its aggressive riffs, hard rhythms and provocative lyrics. But what does this music do to our psyche? Many studies examine the effects on emotions, cognitive processes and even animals.

Emotions: aggression or liberation?

It is often said that heavy metal promotes aggression. However, studies such as those by Sharman & Dingle (2015) show the opposite:

  • Listeners use the music to regulate emotions.

  • Agressive sounds help to reduce anger and negative feelings.

  • Result: improved mood instad of aggression

Metal and brain performance

It is not only emotions that benefit from Metal - cognitive processes are also influenced:

  • Study by Thompson et al. (2001):
  • Complex rythems increase attention and concentration.
  • Metal activates the sympathetic nervous system in a similar way to coffee or energy

Experiments:People and Metal

  1. Aggression control:

    • Gowensmith & Bloom (1997): No increase in aggression.
    • Listeners feel liberated and more positive

  2. Pain tolerance:

    • Mitchell et al. (2006): Metal increases the pain threshold

Animal Studys:

  1. Rats (Kogan et al. , 2012)

    • "Due to the metal music played, the rats retreated and sought shelter in their cages."

  2. Diary cows (Alworth et al., 2012)

    • The cowas Produced less milk, when they listend to metal.

    3. →Metal has a stessful effect on animals.

Social aspects: Community and rsilience

Havy metal fans experience:
  • Strong comunity & sence of belonging
  • Better strategies for dealing with psychological stess, due to the open language of metal. (North & Hargreaves, 2005)

Conclusion:

Metal is more than just Music, because:
  • it supports the emotion regulation.
  • promotes cognitive performance
  • Strengthens psychological resilience.

While Metal has positive effects on Humans, it can cause stress for animals
